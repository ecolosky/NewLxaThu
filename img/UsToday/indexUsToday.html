Roger Grice at Lambda Chi Headquarters in June 2007 to receive training for the rollout of Lambda Chi's True Brother initiative. The rollout will happen in July at the Leadership Seminar in Memphis. The purpose of the True Brother initiative is to return the fraternity to its value-based roots--the things that we all learned (and memorized while doing pushups) when we were in school. Headquarters is an amazing place--a living museum of Lambda Chi Alpha's (and Theta Kappa Nu's) history.

On Sunday, April 1, 2007, there was a Rock 'n' Roll Reunion Concert at the old Brooklyn Paramount Theater at the corner of Flatbush and DeKalb Avenues. I had been to one of the first concerts that Alan Freed held there in 1957. I made the trek into Brooklyn to attend the reunion concert. It was great.

Here's what the old Brooklyn Paramount looks like today--it's the gym for LIU.

Here's the announcement sign on the Flatbush Avenue side of the building.
Here's the program card that they handed out. It's a replication of the ad that appeared in the NY Daily News. What a line-up of acts. What memories!
Here's the back of the handout with details and costs. I got a seat in the bleachers (the old balcony)--all that was left for the 2:30 show by the time I got around to ordering.
Here's a shot of the program finale. My camera does not do a great job of shooting long-range in the dark. I took a lot of shots during the concert, but most of them are too dark to be recognizable.
No trip to the Brooklyn Paramount (actually, no trip to Brooklyn) would be complete without a stop at Junior's--right across Flatbush Avenue from the old Brooklyn Paramount. I stopped there before the concert for lunch: hamburger, chocolate egg creme, and (of course) a slice of Junior's cheesecake.

On the wall by the lunch counter is a shrine to Mary, the waitress who retired recently after 50 years of working at Junior's. That means she must have been there when we were at Poly. Does anyone recognize her?
